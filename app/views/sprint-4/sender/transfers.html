What is a transfer?

You can send spare levy funds in your account by transferring up to 25% of your previous year's funds to another business, to pay for their apprenticeship training. The 25% is known as your transfer allowance.

Who can send and receive a transfer?

You can make a transfer to any business of your choice.

Levy payers can receive a transfer from another levy payer, but you cannot do both at the same time. You can only be a sender or receiver of a transfer at any one time.

By making a transfer of funds, you are only responsible for paying the cost of apprenticeship training for another business with that transfer.

The business receiving the transfer is responsible for everything else related to that apprenticeship.

Deciding who to transfer funding to

There are 2 ways to make a transfer:

  • transfer funding to a business you know by making a connection with them on the apprenticeship service
  • create a public funding pledge which is advertised online so that businesses can apply to you for funding

You can use both options and manage it all in your apprenticeship service account.

Anyone receiving a transfer will also need an apprenticeship service account.

Managing transfers across financial years

When you make a transfer, you commit to funding for the entire duration of the apprenticeship.

This usually means you are committing funding from your current transfers allowance, and from allowances in future financial years.

Your 'committed to transfers' amount shows what you have agreed to transfer in this financial year. It breaks down into 2 amounts:

  • approved funding in principle
  • approved funding where the apprentices and a start date have been added

Your pledge can now be viewed publicly, so that businesses can apply for funding.

You can review, approve and reject applications in your account.

You can create multiple pledges with different criteria.

You can share your pledge link with organisations that you work with to promote the pledge to help generate more applications.

Some partner organisations such as local authorities, skills bodies and training providers, may be able to offer support to the businesses that apply for pledges and help them setting up their apprenticeship service account.

You could also offer support to any businesses you decide to transfer funding to if they are new to working with apprentices.
